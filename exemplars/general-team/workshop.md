# Q3 roadmap workshop

We have twenty ideas and room for six. Today we decide which six, together.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Today we turn twenty ideas into a ranked Q3 roadmap of six.

The backlog has twenty candidate initiatives and we can staff six next quarter. This workshop exists to make that cut together, with shared criteria, so the roadmap is something the whole team chose — not something handed down. By 5pm we leave with a ranked six.

---

<!-- _class: agenda -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## How the afternoon runs.

1. Frame — the goal and the constraints
2. Activity one — score the ideas
3. Activity two — place them on the grid
4. Decide and assign next steps

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## Frame: the constraint is six slots, and they're real.

We have three squads for one quarter — six initiatives, no more. The job today isn't to argue everything is important; it's to choose. We'll use two shared criteria so the choice is consistent and we can explain it to the rest of the company.

- Six slots is fixed: adding a seventh means cutting a sixth.
- We score against impact and effort, nothing else.

---

<!-- _class: list-criteria -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## The two criteria we score every idea against.

1. Customer impact
   - How much does this move a metric a customer would notice and pay for?
2. Effort to ship
   - How many squad-weeks to a shippable version, including the unglamorous parts?
3. Strategic fit
   - Does it build toward the platform direction, or pull sideways?

---

<!-- _class: cards-grid -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Activity one: the four idea clusters on the table.

- Performance
  - Faster load times and a smaller app — broad impact, moderate effort.
- Collaboration
  - Real-time editing and comments — high impact, high effort.
- Admin controls
  - Roles, audit logs, SSO — unblocks enterprise, medium effort.
- Polish
  - Dozens of small fixes — low individual impact, low effort each.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## How activity one works: dot-vote, then discuss the outliers.

Each of you gets five dots to place across the twenty ideas on the wall. We tally, then we only discuss the ideas where scores disagree sharply — that's where the real conversation is. Agreement we accept; disagreement we explore.

- Vote silently first, so the loudest voice doesn't anchor the room.
- We timebox each outlier discussion to four minutes.

---

<!-- _class: matrix-2x2 -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Activity two: place each cluster by impact and effort.

- **Low impact · Low effort.**
  - Polish fixes — fill spare capacity
- **Low impact · High effort.**
  - Legacy migrations — defer
- **High impact · Low effort.**
  - Performance wins — admin controls
- **High impact · High effort.**
  - Real-time collaboration — the flagship bet

---

<!-- _class: list-steps -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## How we get from the grid to a committed roadmap.

1. Pick the quick wins — Everything in the high-impact, low-effort cell goes in first; it's the cheapest value.
2. Choose one big bet — We commit to exactly one high-impact, high-effort flagship, not two.
3. Fill remaining slots — We use the leftover capacity on the next-best ideas until six slots are full.
4. Name what we cut — We write down what didn't make it and why, so the cut is honest and revisitable.

---

<!-- _class: list-steps -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Next steps: who does what before Monday.

1. Write the roadmap — The facilitator turns today's six into a one-page roadmap by Friday.
2. Pressure-test effort — Each squad lead sizes their initiatives and flags any that doubled.
3. Share and confirm — We circulate to the wider team for a 48-hour objection window before it's locked.
