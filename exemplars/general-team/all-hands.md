## A year ago we were one product; today we are a platform.

Twelve months ago Meridian was a single scheduling tool with a churn problem. This quarter we crossed 10,000 paying teams on what is now a platform. This all-hands is about how we got here together — and the one big push that comes next.

## The turning point: we stopped patching churn and rebuilt onboarding.

Last summer one in five new teams left within a month. Instead of more retention emails, we rebuilt the first-week experience from scratch. That single bet changed the trajectory of the whole company.

- The new onboarding cut first-month churn nearly in half.
- It freed the growth team to invest in expansion instead of leak-plugging.

## What we are asking of each other for the rest of the year.

The ecosystem push only works if every team treats developers and partners as first-class users. That means shipping the public API with the same care we give our own product, and saying no to the work that doesn't serve the platform.

- Protect the API roadmap from one-off custom requests.
- Default to building on the platform, not around it.
