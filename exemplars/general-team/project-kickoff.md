# Project Northstar

Consolidating four sales tools into one CRM by year-end — here is the charter.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## We are consolidating four sales tools into one CRM by 19 December.

Sales runs on four disconnected tools today. Northstar replaces them with a single CRM so reps stop re-keying data and leadership gets one source of pipeline truth. This kickoff sets the charter: the why, the scope, the team, and the first decision.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## The problem: four tools, no single view, hours lost to re-entry.

Reps maintain the same account in a CRM, a quoting tool, a spreadsheet, and an email plugin. Nothing syncs. Forecasts are reconciled by hand every Friday, and no one trusts the number the same way twice.

- The sales team estimates six hours per rep per week lost to duplicate data entry.
- Three of the last four board forecasts were revised after the fact.

---

<!-- _class: big-number -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

`The cost of fragmentation`

- 6 hrs
  - lost per rep, per week, to duplicate entry — across 90 reps, that is a full team doing nothing but re-typing.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## The charter: one CRM, one data model, live by 19 December.

Northstar delivers a single CRM with a unified account model, migrated history, and native quoting — replacing all four legacy tools. Success is measured by adoption and forecast trust, not by go-live alone.

- In scope: accounts, opportunities, quoting, and reporting.
- Out of scope: marketing automation and support ticketing — phase two.

---

<!-- _class: list-criteria -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## What "done" must satisfy before we decommission anything.

1. Single source of truth
   - Every account, opportunity, and quote lives in one system with no shadow spreadsheets.
2. Clean migration
   - All open opportunities and two years of closed history move with zero data loss.
3. Reps actually use it
   - 90% weekly active adoption across the sales org within four weeks of launch.
4. Forecast trust restored
   - Leadership reads one pipeline number that reconciles without manual adjustment.

<!-- _class: decision -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## First decision: freeze the data model before any build starts.

- Freeze the schema first
  - Six weeks of design up front prevents months of rework downstream.
- Build and refactor in parallel
  - Feels faster early, but every schema change ripples through migration and quoting.
