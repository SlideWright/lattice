`Slips · what happened`

## The story behind the two items that didn't land.

1. `2026 Feb` Invoicing v2 scoped `decision`
   - The tax-compliance requirements were larger than the original estimate.
2. `2026 Apr` Invoicing v2 paused `at-risk`
   - A dependency on the new billing provider slipped under us.
3. `2026 May` Scheduled exports cut `deferred`
   - Deprioritized to protect the billing date once it was clearly at risk.
4. `2026 Jun` Both rescheduled into Q3 `on-track`
   - Replanned with the provider dependency now resolved.

## What to keep doing and what to change, going into H2.

- **Worked · Keep.**
  - Tight sprint commitments held velocity steady
- **Worked · Keep.**
  - Shipping search and mobile in small increments
- **Struggled · Change.**
  - Estimating compliance-heavy work too lightly
- **Struggled · Fix first.**
  - Taking hard dependencies on unproven vendors

---

<!-- _class: list-criteria -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## The three changes we're carrying into H2 planning.

1. Spike compliance work first
   - Any feature touching tax or legal gets a research spike before it's committed.
2. De-risk vendor dependencies
   - No roadmap date depends on an unproven vendor without a fallback.
3. Commit to six, not seven
   - Leave headroom so one slip doesn't cascade across the plan.
