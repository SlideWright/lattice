# Harvest Table

We rescue surplus food and turn it into hot meals for neighbors who need them.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## In our city, good food gets thrown away while too many people go to bed hungry.

Harvest Table closes that gap. We recover surplus food from grocers and farms, cook it into balanced meals in our community kitchens, and serve it hot — alongside take-home boxes for the days in between.

- Last year alone, our region wasted enough food to feed every hungry household twice over.
- We turn that surplus into dignity: a warm plate, a familiar face, a place to belong.

## 2025 was the year Harvest Table grew from one kitchen to two — and never missed a meal.

We opened our Eastside kitchen in March, grew our volunteer corps to 340, and added two refrigerated trucks. Growth never came at the cost of reliability: we served hot meals every single day of the year.

- A second kitchen cut our average delivery distance nearly in half.
- 340 volunteers gave more than 41,000 hours across rescue, cooking, and the serving line.

`What makes the model work`

## Four commitments behind every meal we serve.

1. Rescue first
   - We buy almost nothing — surplus food from grocers and farms feeds the line.
2. Cook from scratch
   - Trained chefs turn donations into balanced, culturally familiar meals.
3. Serve with dignity
   - A seat, a plate, and a name — never a number in a line.
4. Account for every dollar
   - Audited books and 94% to programs keep donor trust earned, not assumed.

## We are ready to grow — the kitchens are mapped, the volunteers are waiting.

Two more kitchens take us from 412,000 meals to 600,000. Each new site needs a lead chef, a refrigerated truck, and a year of operating support; the surplus food and the volunteers, our communities already supply.

- A funded kitchen reaches its first hot line within 90 days of opening its doors.
