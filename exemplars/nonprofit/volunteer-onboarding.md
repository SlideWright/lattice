## Welcome — you just joined a team of 340 people who show up for animals.

Paws & People is a no-kill shelter and adoption center on Cedar Falls' east side. We take in dogs, cats, and the occasional rabbit nobody else will, and we keep them safe until the right family arrives.

- We've run on volunteers and donations since 2009 — no city funding, no shortcuts.
- Most of what gets done here, gets done by someone exactly like you.

## Our mission is simple — no healthy or treatable animal is ever turned away.

Last year we cared for 2,100 animals and found homes for 1,650 of them. The rest are still with us, getting medical care, training, and the daily attention that makes them adoptable.

- "No-kill" only works when enough hands share the daily load — feeding, walking, comforting.
- An animal that's walked, brushed, and talked to shows better and goes home faster.

## The animals you'll meet are mostly ordinary pets who hit a hard stretch.

On any given day we house around 90 animals. Few are here because something is wrong with them — most arrived because a family moved, lost a home, or simply couldn't keep up.

- Roughly 60 dogs and 30 cats, plus the occasional rabbit, guinea pig, or bonded pair.
- Every animal is vetted, vaccinated, and temperament-noted before it ever meets a volunteer.

## A typical day here runs on three shifts — and every shift needs hands.

The shelter opens at 7 a.m. for morning care and closes after the evening clean. Volunteers pick the shifts that fit their week; there's no minimum, though most settle into one regular slot.

- Morning `7–10 a.m.` — feeding, kennel cleaning, and the first dog walks of the day.
- Midday `11 a.m.–2 p.m.` — adoption-floor support, cat socializing, laundry.
- Evening `4–7 p.m.` — final walks, enrichment, and settling everyone for the night.

## Three steps to your first shift.

1. Orientation
   - Complete this session and sign the volunteer agreement and liability waiver.
2. Shadow shift
   - Pair with an experienced volunteer for one two-hour shift to learn the routine.
3. Solo ready
   - Pick up your badge and start booking your own shifts in the volunteer portal.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## Most people are booking solo shifts within their first two weeks.

There's no test and no rush. You'll learn the routine by doing it alongside someone who's done it a hundred times, and you move to solo shifts the moment you feel ready — not before.

- Bring questions, not experience — every coordinator started exactly where you are now.
- If a task isn't for you, say so; there's always another way to help the animals.

---

<!-- _class: content -->

## Booking a shift takes about a minute, all from your phone.

Once your badge is active, the volunteer portal shows every open slot for the next two weeks. Claim one, swap one, or cancel — just give us four hours' notice so we can backfill the floor.

- Set a recurring slot and it auto-books each week; you'll get a text reminder the night before.
- No-shows happen — tell Priya and we'll never make it a thing.

## A few house rules that keep both you and the animals safe.

These aren't bureaucracy — every one comes from something we learned the hard way. Read them once and they'll become second nature by your second shift.

- Never enter a kennel with a red card on the door without a staff member present.
- Wash hands between animals, and always sign in and out so we know who's on the floor.
- If an animal frightens you, step back and call a coordinator — that's the right move, not a failure.
