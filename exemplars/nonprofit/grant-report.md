# Bridgeway Workforce

Two years and $750K later, more adults are in healthcare careers than we promised.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## The grant funded a two-year run to train and place Dayton adults in healthcare jobs.

The Hartwell Foundation's $750,000 grant ran July 2024 through June 2026. It funded the Bridgeway Healthcare Career Track — recruiting, training, certifying, and placing un- and under-employed adults into entry-level clinical roles.

- Goal: enroll 200 adults, certify them, place 75% in living-wage jobs.
- This report covers the full grant period against those commitments.

## Recruitment reached the neighborhoods the grant prioritized.

We committed to drawing enrollees from Dayton's three highest-unemployment ZIP codes. Community partners — two churches, the public library, and the county jobs center — drove the bulk of qualified referrals.

- 64% of the 218 enrollees came from the target ZIPs, ahead of our 50% goal.
- 71% were women; 58% were parents supporting at least one child.

## The remaining $8K carries into a no-cost extension.

We spent $742K of the $750K award. The unspent $8K is committed to the final independent evaluation, which lands in Q3 2026 — one quarter past the grant's close.

- Admin held at 12%, below the 15% cap the grant allowed.
- No funds were reallocated across budget lines without prior approval.

## One employer partner stalled, and one cohort ran small.

We promised ten employer partners and landed eight; a regional system paused hiring during a merger. Our winter 2025 cohort enrolled 41 against a 55-seat plan after a transit-route cut.

- We backfilled placements through the eight active partners — no graduate went unplaced.
- We restored cohort size in spring once a shuttle stipend was added.
