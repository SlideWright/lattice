# Riverstone Reads

A child who can't read by fourth grade rarely catches up. We make sure they do.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## In our city, two of every three low-income third graders read below grade level.

Riverstone Reads runs daily one-on-one tutoring inside public elementary schools, pairing a trained volunteer with the same child all year. We work where the children already are, with the teachers who already know them.

- Reading on grade level by fourth grade is the single strongest predictor of high-school graduation.
- A child who falls behind early almost never closes the gap without help.

## Meet Daniela — she started second grade unable to read a single sentence.

When Daniela joined Lincoln Elementary, she scored in the 9th percentile for reading and hid at the back of the room during story time. Her teacher flagged her for Riverstone Reads in October.

- She was paired with Marcus, a retired postal worker, for 30 minutes every school day.
- By spring she was reading chapter books aloud — and raising her hand.

## Daniela is not the exception. She is what our model does at scale.

The research on early literacy is settled: consistent, relationship-based tutoring moves children faster than any classroom intervention alone. Our job is to deliver that consistency without fail, to every child we take on.

- Same tutor, same child, every school day — relationship is the active ingredient.
- Teachers refer; we never replace them, we multiply their reach.
