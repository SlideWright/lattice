# Lakeside Community Health

In Eastside, a family waits three weeks for a doctor and drives an hour for a dentist. We're building the clinic that ends that wait.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Eastside has 28,000 residents and not one full-service health clinic.

For two decades, Lakeside Community Health has run pop-up clinics out of a converted school bus and a rented church basement. Demand has long outgrown the space. The neighborhood needs a permanent home for care.

- The nearest clinic taking new Medicaid patients is 11 miles away, across two bus transfers.
- One in three Eastside children has never seen a dentist.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## Meet the Reyes family — they are why this clinic cannot wait.

Marisol Reyes brought her son Diego to our bus clinic with a toothache he'd had for months. We could treat the pain but had no chair to do the extraction. She took an unpaid day off work and three buses to a clinic that could.

- Diego is one of 4,200 Eastside children with no dental home.
- A permanent clinic means same-day care, not a day's lost wages.

## The plan is finished, the land is secured — we are funding the building.

We hold a 99-year lease on the old Eastside firehouse lot, the architectural plans are approved, and the county has committed operating support once we open. The capital campaign funds construction, equipment, and the first year of care.

- $5.4M builds and outfits the clinic; $2.6M equips dental, exam, and pharmacy.
- Every operating dollar after opening is already underwritten by county and Medicaid reimbursement.
