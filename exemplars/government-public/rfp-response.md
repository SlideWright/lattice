`RFP DPR-2027-04 · Oral presentation`

# Permitting System Replacement

Our proposal to replace the county's 22-year-old building-permit system within 18 months.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## We understand the goal: cut permit turnaround from weeks to days, without a risky big-bang cutover.

The county's permitting backlog is a constituent-service problem before it is a technology one. Our approach modernises in phases, keeps the legacy system live until each module is proven, and ships a measurable turnaround improvement in the first six months.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## We have read the problem behind the requirements.

The RFP asks for a new system. The real requirement is faster, more predictable permits for residents and contractors, delivered without the failed cutover that derailed the 2021 attempt. Our proposal is shaped to that outcome, not just the feature list.

---

<!-- _class: list-criteria -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## How our approach meets each evaluation criterion.

1. Phased, low-risk delivery
   - Module-by-module rollout with the legacy system live as fallback until each phase is proven.
2. Demonstrated turnaround gain
   - A measurable reduction in permit cycle time inside the first two phases, not at the end.
3. Open, portable architecture
   - Standards-based and exportable; no proprietary lock-in to us as a vendor.
4. Accessibility & multilingual
   - WCAG 2.1 AA and three-language intake from first release.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## Our approach: ship value in phases, never a big-bang cutover.

We deliver the highest-volume permit types first, run them in parallel with the legacy system, and migrate the rest only once each module is proven in production. The 2021 attempt failed on a single cutover; ours cannot fail that way.

## The 18-month delivery plan, by workstream.

- Discovery & design
  - Process mapping `Q3 → Q4` `done`
  - Solution design `Q4 → Q1` `live`
- Core platform
  - Residential permits `Q1 → Q2` `live`
  - Commercial permits `Q2 → Q3` `at-risk`
  - Inspections module `Q3 → Q4`
- Migration & cutover
  - Parallel run `Q2 → Q4`
  - Legacy retirement `Q4`
- Enablement
  - Staff training `Q1 → Q3`
  - Public launch `Q3 → Q4`

## Why the phased approach is the risk story, not a slower one.

A big-bang cutover concentrates all risk on one date — the failure mode of the 2021 attempt. Phasing spreads risk across modules, each independently reversible. The county sees benefit in month six and never bets the whole service on a single go-live.
