`Community town hall · Spring 2027`

# Where Your Tax Dollars Went

A plain accounting of the city budget, and what the new street-paving levy is buying.

---

<!-- _class: big-number -->
<!-- tier: short -->

`This year's city budget`

- $142M
  - in total spending — about $1,840 for every resident of Granger.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Three services take two of every three dollars you pay.

Public safety, streets and transit, and parks and libraries account for 67% of the budget. The rest covers water, planning, administration, and debt service. This town hall walks through where the money goes and what changed this year.

## This year's change: the street-paving levy you approved.

Last November residents approved a five-year street levy. This is the first year it is in effect, adding $18M dedicated entirely to repaving the streets the city's own survey rated in poor condition.

## What the street levy is buying, in its first year.

1. 64 mi

## How a street gets picked for repaving.

The city surveys every street on a pavement-condition index from 0 to 100. Streets scoring below 40 are rated poor and enter the queue, worst-first, balanced so no neighbourhood waits more than the levy's five-year span.

- The full street-by-street list is posted on the city website.
- Residents can look up their own street's score and queue position.

## What we are asking of you tonight.

We want your priorities for next year's budget. Three things are on the table: extending library hours, adding a second crew to speed up paving, and a new crosswalk-safety program. Your input shapes what the council votes on in June.

## How to stay involved after tonight.

The draft budget is open for comment through May. You can comment online, speak at the two budget hearings, or email your council member directly. Every comment received is logged and answered before the June vote.

- Budget hearings: 12 May and 26 May, 7 p.m., City Hall.
- Comment portal and full budget: granger.gov.example/budget.
