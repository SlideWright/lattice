`Public hearing · Case ZA-2027-118`

# Riverside Mixed-Use Rezoning

The department's testimony on the application to rezone the 14-acre former rail yard from industrial to mixed-use.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## The department's position: approve the rezoning, with three binding conditions.

The application meets the comprehensive plan's housing and transit goals. We recommend approval conditioned on affordability set-asides, riverfront access, and traffic mitigation — the three matters where the as-filed plan falls short of policy.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## The site has sat vacant for eleven years since the rail yard closed.

The 14-acre parcel was rezoned industrial in 1974 and has produced no jobs or housing since the yard ceased operations in 2016. The comprehensive plan designates this corridor for transit-oriented mixed-use.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## What the applicant proposes to build.

A phased development of 620 housing units, 84,000 square feet of ground-floor retail, and a riverfront public esplanade, anchored to the light-rail station opening two blocks north in 2029.

- Density is within the corridor's adopted maximum.
- Phase one breaks ground 18 months after approval.

---

<!-- _class: list-criteria -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## What the comprehensive plan requires of any approval here.

1. Affordability set-aside
   - At least 15% of units must be affordable at 60% of area median income.
2. Public riverfront access
   - Continuous public access along the full river frontage, dedicated in perpetuity.
3. Traffic mitigation
   - Intersection improvements sized to the development's projected peak trips.
4. Transit integration
   - A direct pedestrian connection to the future light-rail platform.

## Where the as-filed plan falls short of policy.

The application meets the density and transit goals but proposes only 9% affordable units, treats riverfront access as a private amenity, and omits the intersection study the traffic threshold requires. Each gap is the basis for a condition.

---

<!-- tier: full -->

## The summary of public comment received during the review period.

1. Comments in support `214`
   - Cited housing need, jobs, and reuse of vacant land.
2. Comments opposed `88`
   - Cited construction traffic and shadow on the adjacent neighbourhood.
3. Comments conditional `132`
   - Supported the project if affordability and river access were guaranteed.
4. Neighbourhood council
   - Voted 6–1 to support, contingent on the three conditions.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## The opposition's traffic concern is real, and the condition answers it.

Construction-phase traffic on the residential approach is the most-cited objection. The proposed condition requires a phased haul-route plan and off-peak delivery windows, with a bonded penalty for violations — the standard mechanism applied to comparable corridor projects.
