`Inter-agency coordination · April 2027`

# Coastal Flood Response

Standing up a joint command for the Bayshore levee district before the autumn storm season.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## The ask: agree a single joint command and a shared funding split by 1 June.

Four agencies hold pieces of the Bayshore flood response and none holds the whole. We propose a standing joint command with one incident commander, a pooled $46M readiness fund, and signed roles before storm season opens.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## Last autumn's response failed on coordination, not capability.

When the King Tide breached the Bayshore levee in November, three agencies deployed pumps to the same district while a fourth waited on an authorisation that never came. The after-action review found no single commander and no shared funding authority.

---

<!-- _class: content -->
<!-- tier: standard -->

## Each agency owns a piece; no one owns the outcome.

The levees, the pumps, the evacuation order, and the recovery funds sit in four different agencies under three different statutes. The gap is not resources — it is the absence of a structure that makes one person accountable for the whole response.

---

<!-- _class: actors -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Who owns what under the proposed joint command.

- Incident command & levee operations `Water District`
  - Single incident commander; owns levee monitoring, breach response, and pump deployment.
- Evacuation & public warning `Emergency Mgmt`
  - Issues evacuation orders and runs the public alert system on the commander's call.
- Funding & procurement `Finance Office`
  - Holds the pooled readiness fund and pre-authorises emergency procurement.
- Recovery & infrastructure `Public Works`
  - Leads post-event debris, road, and levee repair under a standing contract.

---

<!-- _class: actors -->
<!-- tier: full -->

## The supporting roles the command draws on during an activation.

- Forecasting & triggers `Met Service`
  - Provides the tidal and rainfall forecasts that trigger each readiness level.
- Health & shelter `Public Health`
  - Stands up shelters and medical points once an evacuation is ordered.
- Legal authority `County Counsel`
  - Confirms the statutory basis for orders issued under emergency activation.

## The pooled fund is the part that needs a decision today.

The command structure can be chartered administratively, but the $46M readiness fund requires each agency to commit its share into a single pooled account with pre-authorised draw-down. Without that, the next response repeats last autumn's funding stall. This is the item we need agreed in the room.

---

<!-- _class: decision -->
<!-- tier: short -->

## Charter the joint command and pool the readiness fund before 1 June.

- Charter the joint command
  - Puts one accountable commander over the whole response, closing last year's gap.
- Why not keep four mandates
  - The after-action review traced the breach failure directly to divided command.
- Why not delay to next cycle
  - A delayed charter misses the September drill and the next storm season.
