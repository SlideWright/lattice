A chart deserves the whole slide; the label can wait at the bottom. `cover` takes any chart edge-to-edge and reflows the title and one closing line into a caption band — the same shelf the chart panels already use.

## `cover` — the chart owns the canvas, the caption owns the floor.

Distinct from `image full`: that's for photographs; `cover` is for charts (and the occasional statement).

<!-- _footer: "Content cover · the distinction" -->

On a plain content slide, `cover` keeps the body's standard margins so prose stays readable, and only the caption band breaks out to the edges. Charts fill the bleed; content keeps its gutters. Same modifier, the layout decides.

## Content keeps its margins; only charts fill the bleed.

The trailing line is clamped to two, so the caption never grows a third.
