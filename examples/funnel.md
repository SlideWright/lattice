# Watch where the flow leaks.

`New component · evidence · canvas · series`

The pipeline slide for any narrowing flow — a sales or conversion funnel, a hiring pipeline, a grant or donor pipeline. Band width tracks each stage's value; the stage-to-stage conversion rate prints in the gaps, so the leak is the read.

---

<!-- _class: content -->

## Same shape, wherever the pipeline narrows.

A funnel earns its trapezoids when every stage is a strict subset of the one above it and the audience needs to see *which step* loses the most.

- Author stages in flow order, widest first: `- Visitors \`12,000\``. The largest value sets full width; the rest scale to it.
- Three to seven stages read best. Two is just a ratio — use a `big-number`. A category breakdown that isn't a subset belongs in a `piechart`.
