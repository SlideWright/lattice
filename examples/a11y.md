# Meaning that survives colour blindness.

One front-matter line — `accessibility: deuteranopia` — and the deck stops
relying on hue alone. Status reads by **shape**, categories by **texture**.

---

<!-- _class: silent -->

`The problem`

## ~8% of men can't separate your categories by colour.

Under deuteranopia, red↔green collapse; the pale categorical fills a deck
leans on drop to **one or two** distinguishable colours. A palette can't fix
that — the information has to ride a second channel.

`The two redundant channels`

## Shape carries what colour can't.

`✓ ! ✗` glyphs prefix every status pill, and each categorical slot gets a
distinct **texture** — diagonal, dots, grid, chevron, rings, checker — so a
timeline or pie reads in pure greyscale, or to any colour-blind viewer.

---



<!-- _class: silent -->

`On diagrams`

## A texture per categorical slot.

On a timeline, journey, kanban, or pie, every category fill carries its colour
**and** a distinct pattern — diagonal, dots, grid, chevron, rings, checker. Read
the deck in greyscale, or hand it to a colour-blind viewer, and the categories
still pull apart. (Diagram textures render in the full engine; this demo shows
the status-pill channel.)
